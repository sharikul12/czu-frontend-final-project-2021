About Bandarban: Bandarban (Bengali: বান্দরবান ) is a district in Bangladesh's Chittagong Division, located in the south-east. It is one of Bangladesh's three hill districts and a part of the Chittagong Hill Tracts, along with Rangamati and Khagrachhari districts. The Bandarban district's headquarters is Bandarban city. Bandarban district (4,479 km2) is not just the country's most remote but also the least populous district (population 292,900). At Bandarban Cantonment, there is an army contingent.


The Buddhist temple of Bangladesh, the Buddha Dhatu Jadi, is located near Balaghata, 4 kilometers from the town. Every year, a large number of tourists visit this location. This Theravada Buddhist temple is built entirely in the manner of Southeast Asia and holds Bangladesh's second largest Buddha statue. Another popular tourist destination is the Shoilo Propat waterfall in Milanchari. The extremely noteworthy Rajvihar (royal vihar) in Jadipara and the Ujanipara Bhihar are among the several Buddhist temples, known as kyang in local tongue, and bhihars in the town. Bawm villages in the Chimbuk area, as well as Mru villages a bit further out, are both within a day's drive of the town. Jibanna's Prantik Lake
